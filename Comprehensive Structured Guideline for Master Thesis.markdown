# Comprehensive Structured Guideline for Master Thesis

This guideline integrates your university's requirements with the *Deutsche Gesellschaft für Psychologie* (DGP) *Richtlinien zur Manuskriptgestaltung* (2019). University guidelines take precedence in case of conflicts, while DGP guidelines provide supplementary details.

---

## 1. Language

- The thesis will be written in English.

---

## 2. Structure

The thesis must follow this structure, as mandated by the university, with additional details from DGP guidelines:

### 2.1 Title Page

- **Content**: Include the thesis title, your name, university name, department, degree program, supervisor’s name, and submission date.
- **Formatting**: Follow DGP recommendations for a clear and professional layout (refer to DGP Section 1.2).

### 2.2 Abstract

- **Purpose**: Provide a concise summary of the thesis, including the research question, methods, results, and conclusions.
- **Length**: Limit to 250 words or fewer (DGP Section 1.3).
- **Placement**: Place immediately after the title page.

### 2.3 Introduction/Theory Part

- **Content**: Provide background information, review relevant literature, and establish the theoretical foundation for your research.
- **Purpose**: Set the context for your research question and hypotheses (DGP Section 1.4).

### 2.4 Research Question and Hypotheses

- **Content**: Clearly state your research question and hypotheses.
- **Requirements**: Ensure they are specific, measurable, and testable (DGP Section 1.4).

### 2.5 Method

- **Subsections**:
  - **Research Design**: Specify the study type (e.g., experimental, observational).
  - **Participants**: Describe sampling methods and participant characteristics.
  - **Materials**: List instruments and tools used.
  - **Procedures**: Outline the steps followed in your research.
- **Detail**: Provide enough information for replication (DGP Section 1.5).

### 2.6 Results

- **Content**: Present findings using appropriate statistical analyses.
- **Visual Aids**: Use tables and figures as needed, formatted per APA guidelines (DGP Section 1.6).
- **Clarity**: Focus on key results without interpretation.

### 2.7 Discussion

- **Content**:
  - Interpret results in relation to the research question and hypotheses.
  - Discuss implications and limitations.
  - Suggest future research directions.
- **Depth**: Balance conciseness with thorough analysis (DGP Section 1.7).

### 2.8 References

- **Content**: List all cited sources.
- **Formatting**: Follow APA style (2020) for in-text citations and the reference list (DGP Section 7-10).

### 2.9 Appendices

- **Content**: Include supplementary materials (e.g., raw data, questionnaires).
- **Labeling**: Use letters (e.g., Appendix A, Appendix B) (DGP Section 1.8).

---

## 3. Formatting Requirements

Adhere to these mandatory university specifications, supplemented by DGP recommendations:

- **Font Size**: 11 or 12.
- **Font Type**: Arial or Calibri.
- **Line Spacing**: 1.3.
- **Margins**:
  - Right: 2.5 cm
  - Left: 3 cm
  - Top: 2.5 cm
  - Bottom: 3 cm
- **Page Numbers**: Bottom right, formatted as "current page/total pages" (e.g., 1/39).
- **Page Limit**: Maximum 40 pages (double-sided printing), excluding Abstract, References, and Appendices.
- **APA Style**: Follow APA (2020) guidelines for manuscript preparation (e.g., headings, citations, tables).

**Note**: Failure to follow these formatting rules may result in rejection and a request for reformatting.

---

## 4. Writing Style

- **Style**: Use a scientific writing style that is clear, concise, and focused on essential content.
- **Guidelines**:
  - Avoid lengthy descriptions, excessive theory elaboration, or complex sentence structures.
  - Use simple, precise sentences to convey key points effectively (University & DGP Section 2.4).
- **Gender-Inclusive Language**: Follow DGP recommendations for gender-neutral phrasing where applicable (DGP Section 2.4).

---

## 5. Planning

- **Outline**: Create an initial outline at the start to structure your thesis and manage time effectively.
- **Time Management**: Account for the effort required to write concisely; writing is not a quick task.

---

## 6. Ethical Considerations

- **Compliance**: Ensure adherence to ethical guidelines (e.g., informed consent, confidentiality) as per DGP recommendations (DGP Section 1.9 & 2018 Ethical Guidelines).

---

## 7. Additional DGP Recommendations

These enhance your thesis where university guidelines are silent:

- **Tables and Figures**:
  - Use to present data visually, following APA (2020) formatting (DGP Sections 5-6).
  - Place titles and legends directly below figures (DGP Section 6.4).
- **Footnotes**: Use sparingly, only when necessary (DGP Section 2.2).
- **Headings**: Use a consistent APA-style heading structure (DGP Section 3.1).
- **Proofreading**: Ensure the thesis is free of grammatical errors and typos (DGP Section 2.3).

---

## 8. Submission Tips

- **Double-Check**: Verify compliance with all university formatting and content requirements before submission.
- **Relevance**: Despite the 40-page limit, include all critical information concisely.

---

By following this guideline, your master thesis will meet university standards while benefiting from the detailed structure and professionalism of the DGP recommendations. If you need clarification on any section, feel free to ask!
