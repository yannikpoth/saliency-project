This study investigates how salient audiovisual feedback influences learning rates and choice behavior in a reinforcement learning framework. Participants completed a restless two-armed bandit task where salient cues were paired with rewards following a variable reinforcement pattern. The research aims to understand how these cues affect learning processes, decision-making, and reward evaluation, with potential implications for adaptive and maladaptive behaviors. Data analysis will employ hierarchical Bayesian reinforcement learning models to quantify learning dynamics and examine individual differences in the impact of salient cues.
